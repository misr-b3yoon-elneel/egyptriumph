Bahr El Baqar Water Treatment Plant

World's Largest Water Treatment Plant

The Bahr El Baqar Water Treatment Plant is a monumental achievement in Egypt's water infrastructure, with capacity to treat 5.6 million cubic meters of agricultural drainage water daily.

Key Features:

  • World's largest water treatment plant by capacity
  • Recycles agricultural drainage water for irrigation
  • Supports reclamation of 400,000 acres of desert land
  • Uses advanced multi-stage treatment technology

Cairo Metro Expansion

The Cairo Metro, Africa's first underground transportation system, has undergone massive expansion to serve the growing population of Greater Cairo.

With new lines and modern trains, the metro now transports over 4 million passengers daily, reducing traffic congestion and pollution.

Egyptian Monorail System

Africa's First Monorail

Egypt is building Africa's first monorail system with two lines connecting the New Administrative Capital and 6th of October City to Greater Cairo, revolutionizing urban transportation.

Key Features:

  • Two lines totaling 96.6 km in length
  • 34 fully automated, driverless trains
  • Capacity for 45,000 passengers per hour
  • Reduces traffic congestion and pollution

New Administrative Capital

Egypt's Smart Future City

The New Administrative Capital is a planned city covering 700 square kilometers, designed to alleviate congestion in Cairo and serve as Egypt's new governmental and financial hub.

Key Features:

  • Africa's tallest tower (Iconic Tower at 385m)
  • Smart city with advanced digital infrastructure
  • Green spaces covering 40% of the city area
  • Modern transportation networks including monorail

Roads Network Development

Transforming Egypt's Transportation

Egypt has invested heavily in expanding and modernizing its road network, adding thousands of kilometers of new roads, bridges, and tunnels to connect cities and reduce travel times.

Key Achievements:

  • 7,000+ km of new roads constructed nationwide
  • Dozens of new bridges and interchanges
  • Modern highways connecting all governorates
  • Intelligent transportation systems implemented

Asmarat District Development

Transforming Informal Areas

The Asmarat district represents a major achievement in urban development, transforming informal settlements into modern, integrated communities with full services and infrastructure.

Project Features:

  • 11,000 housing units built in first phase
  • Complete infrastructure including water, sewage, electricity
  • Schools, medical centers, and commercial areas
  • Green spaces and community facilities
