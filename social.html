Hayah Karima Initiative

The "Decent Life" initiative is Egypt's most ambitious rural development project, aiming to transform the lives of over 58 million Egyptians in rural areas.

With investments exceeding EGP 700 billion, the initiative focuses on improving living conditions through infrastructure, healthcare, education, and economic opportunities.

Education Transformation

Before & After: Schools in Egypt

The past decade has seen remarkable improvements in educational infrastructure and quality. From dilapidated buildings to modern learning environments, Egypt's schools have undergone a dramatic transformation.

Before 2014:

  • Overcrowded classrooms (50+ students per class)
  • Lack of basic facilities like labs and libraries
  • Outdated curriculum and teaching methods

After 2024:

  • 14,000+ new classrooms built
  • Smart classrooms with digital learning tools
  • Updated curriculum focusing on critical thinking
  • Teacher training programs for 500,000+ educators

Healthcare Revolution

Before & After: Healthcare in Egypt

Egypt's healthcare system has undergone a comprehensive overhaul, with new hospitals, universal insurance coverage, and groundbreaking public health initiatives.

Before 2014:

  • Limited access to healthcare in rural areas
  • Overcrowded and under-equipped public hospitals
  • High rates of preventable diseases

After 2024:

  • Universal Health Insurance system implemented
  • 125+ new hospitals and 1,500 medical units
  • "100 Million Healthy Lives" screening campaign
  • Specialized cancer treatment centers nationwide
